St Francis of Assisi High school is a Roman Catholic, boarding and Co-educational School. It is situated in Chikomba District, Nharira Communal Area and is about 35km South East of Chivhu Town. The school was established in January 1965 by the Franciscan Friars of the Archdiocese of Harare as a two-stream educational school. Initially it catered for students who wanted to do secondary education up to form two that is, two-year secondary course after standard six. The school then added the ‘O’ level stream and entered the first group of candidates to write G.C.E. O’ level examination in 1971. The total enrolment was about 220 students. However, by 1978 its enrolment had increased to 335 students as the school had been allowed to increase enrolment so as to accommodate students who had been displaced from secondary school that were in war-torn areas which were therefore forced to close due to the War of Liberation of Zimbabwe. The school was forced to close on February 15, 1979 due to the security situation during the War of Liberation. The Head’s office, eight classrooms together with all staff room, all fixtures and furniture, equipment and all records were destroyed in the issuing of fire. The Mission and the school were evacuated without loss of life.
